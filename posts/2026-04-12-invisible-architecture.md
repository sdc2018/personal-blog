## The Pyramids No One Talks About

There is a pyramid of wealth. A pyramid of political power. A pyramid of celebrity. A pyramid of intelligence. Each one has a small elite at the top.

Now here is the thing nobody talks about — the tops of these pyramids are connected. The same families appear in all of them. They socialise together. They marry each other. They go to Bilderberg, Davos, private dinners that never get reported. They help each other get to the top. And they resist outsiders who try to climb.

People who climb these pyramids face huge resistance. But once you cross a certain point, once you follow the unwritten rules and prove your loyalty, you get admitted into the circle. After that you are protected. You are above normal rules.

Jeffrey Epstein was not an accident. He was a feature. A node in a network. His function was to bind elites together through shared secrets. That is how elite networks maintain loyalty — everyone has something to lose, so no one speaks.

## How Old Is This?

This is not a modern problem. This has been going on for centuries.

The European banking families who financed kings and colonies in the 1600s — Rothschilds, Warburgs, Barings — they did not disappear. They converted their wealth into institutional control. Central banks. Insurance. Commodity trading. The Bank of England was founded in 1694. The US Federal Reserve was created in 1913 — but it was designed secretly in 1910 by seven bankers meeting on a private island in Georgia. They came in a private train with curtains drawn. Used only first names. Wrote the legislation themselves. Had a senator introduce it.

This is not conspiracy. This is documented history.

Post-World War II, America took over the apex. Dollar became the reserve currency. Whoever controls the dollar controls global liquidity. Gulf oil money joined in after 1973 — they keep oil in dollars, America provides military protection, and Gulf royals get admitted to the global elite social circle.

And spiritual leaders — they go to Davos, they meet these elites, they speak at their forums. Why? Because to reach global scale you need media access, political protection, financial infrastructure. Elites need them too — a spiritual figure gives legitimacy, keeps masses peaceful, provides a narrative that suffering is karma not exploitation. It is an ancient transaction. Roman emperors funded the Church. Today's elites fund their Davos saints.

## The Machine That Runs on Your Salary

After 1971 when Nixon ended the gold standard, something fundamental changed. Before 1971 — every dollar had to be backed by gold. Money supply had limits. Debt had limits.

After 1971 — money is just paper backed by trust. The government can print unlimited money. And here is what happens when you print money: whoever gets it first benefits most. Banks get it first. They buy assets. Assets rise. By the time the new money reaches you as salary increment — inflation has already eaten it.

This is called the Cantillon Effect. It is the hidden mechanism of wealth transfer.

Now add the market cycle on top of this.

After every crash, assets are cheap. Elites buy at the bottom — they have cash, they have information, they have access to credit. Common people are too scared or too broke to buy. Then the market recovers. Media creates FOMO. Mutual fund advertisements are everywhere. SIP inflows surge. Common man enters late. Elites quietly sell to the common man at the top. Then the next crash comes. Common man panics, sells at the bottom. Wealth has transferred upward. Cycle resets in 7 to 12 years.

2008 is the clearest example. Banks created mortgage products they knew were toxic. Sold them to pension funds — the retirement savings of ordinary people. Crashed. Got bailed out with taxpayer money. Paid record bonuses in 2009. Not one senior banker went to jail.

And the law? The law always arrives after the elites have already moved.

Elite exploits the system. People get angry. Government "acts." But the law is written with elite input through lobbying. By the time the law passes, elites have already shifted to the next thing. The law legitimises their new position and raises barriers for everyone else. The peasant celebrates when the law passes. The elite designed the law.

## The Genius of Psychological Slavery

Physical slavery ended. But something more elegant replaced it.

The person with loan payments cannot strike. Cannot quit. Cannot resist. Debt is the new chain — but he chose it. He signed the loan papers. He opened the trading account. He voted for the politician who promised him growth. It all feels like freedom.

Eduard Bernays — Freud's nephew, hired by American corporations in the 1920s — figured this out. Link self-worth to consumption. Make people feel that what they own is who they are. The result: people work their whole lives to buy things they don't need with money they don't have to impress people they don't like.

And the left-right political divide? That is the master stroke. Keep people fighting about religion, caste, identity, nationalism. Make sure they never look upward at the economic structure above. Both political parties anywhere — are funded by the same apex capital. The fight is theatre. The structure remains untouched.

Antonio Gramsci called this hegemony. The ruling class does not need force when the ruled class has internalised the rulers' values as common sense. The slave believes he is free because he chose his chains.

## What About the Middle Class?

I have tried to build genuine networks of middle class intellectuals. People with real ability and knowledge. And I keep hitting the same wall.

They do not help each other. They do not share opportunities genuinely. They are too busy protecting what they have earned.

And I understand why. They believe in meritocracy more than anyone — because it is how they got where they are. Helping someone laterally feels like diluting their competitive advantage. The IIT entrance exam trained them to see everyone around as a competitor. That wiring does not go away with success.

The elite network compounds across generations. They help each other's children, open doors, share information. The middle class "network" is transactional — it exists only when there is immediate mutual benefit.

This is one of the saddest things I have observed. The people who most need to collaborate are the ones who resist it most.

## Now the Harder Question

All of this — the pyramids, the financial cycles, the psychological traps — it only works because of something inside us.

Think about it. Consumer culture works because it exploits desire. Debt works because it exploits fear. Political division works because it exploits tribal instinct. Social media works because it exploits the need for validation.

These are all ancient forces inside the human being. They existed long before any banker or advertiser discovered them. The outer system did not create them — it simply learned to use them.

So here is the real question: who is actually responsible?

Human beings carry three layers of conditioning.

The first is biological — ancient genetic programming. Fear, aggression, sexual drive, the need for hierarchy. Your amygdala does not know you are sitting in a NY office in 2026. It is still running software designed for a savanna 100,000 years ago.

The second is environmental — family patterns, cultural conditioning, whatever your parents and grandparents went through. Epigenetics now confirms that trauma and belief patterns alter gene expression and are passed down generations. Your grandfather's scarcity anxiety may literally live in your nervous system.

## The Only Real Exit

The discriminative intelligence. The ability to observe your own conditioning rather than being your conditioning.

Viktor Frankl, who survived Nazi concentration camps, said it this way: "Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is our power to choose our response."

That space — that gap — is everything. It is the entire spiritual path compressed into one sentence.

Most people have almost no gap. Stimulus arrives, reaction follows immediately. Someone insults you — anger fires. Someone attractive walks by — desire fires. Market crashes — fear fires. There is no space between the two.

The gap can grow. This is what meditation, self-inquiry, honest reflection, and genuine spiritual practice actually does. Not the Instagram version of spirituality. The real work.

When the gap grows, something remarkable happens. The outer system gradually loses its grip on you. Not because the world changes. But because the hooks find less to catch.

A person who genuinely needs little cannot be leveraged by debt. A person who is not chasing status cannot be manipulated by manufactured aspiration. A person who watches their own fear cannot be stampeded by market panic.

The Upanishads call this Vairagya — dispassion. Not coldness. Not indifference. A deep settledness that does not depend on external conditions.

This is not the ashram option. This is not dropping out of the world. This is the most practical thing I know. The man who needs little from the system has the most freedom within it.

## So Where Does This Leave Us?

I am not saying leave everything and go meditate in the Himalayas. I am a businessman. I have companies, partners, responsibilities, a family. I live inside this system like everyone else.

But I am saying: know the game you are playing. Do not be naive about it.

The game is real. The pyramids are real. The financial cycles are real. The psychological traps are real. Understanding this is not pessimism — it is clarity.

And within that clarity, some things follow naturally:

Hold real assets — land, gold, equity in your own business — not just financial instruments that can be inflated away or crash in cycles. Minimise debt. Every EMI is a degree of freedom you have sold. Build genuine relationships based on trust and mutual respect, not transactional networking. Develop knowledge and judgment — they cannot be confiscated or inflated away.

And do the inner work. Not because it makes you a saint. But because it makes you progressively harder to exploit.

The system runs on unresolved inner conditioning. As the inner resolves, the outer loses its power.

That is the only exit that actually works.
